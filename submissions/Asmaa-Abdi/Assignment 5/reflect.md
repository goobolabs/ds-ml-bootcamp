I built a loan approval classification pipeline. After cleaning data, feature engineering (DebtToIncome, IncomePerYearEmployed), and scaled them using MinMaxScaler, I trained three models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Decision Tree.
Comparison of Models: Sanity Check: Predictions differed; the Decision Tree adapted dynamically to logical splits, while Logistic Regression and Random Forest generated identical, more conservative boundaries due to the small sample size.|| More Realistic: Random Forest is more realistic for banking because credit risk depends on non-linear combinations of factors, not a single straight line.
Understanding Random Forest: It is an ensemble algorithm that builds multiple individual decision trees during training. It combines their outputs and uses a majority vote for the final prediction, which reduces variance and prevents overfitting.
Other Algorithms: Third Classifier: Decision Tree Classifier. |||Pros & Cons: It is highly intuitive and easy to visualize (advantage), but it overfits easily and is very unstable with small changes in training data (limitation).
